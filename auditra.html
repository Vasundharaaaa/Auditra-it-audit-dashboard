Regulation & Compliance Reference
Key frameworks applicable to micro-organizations in India and globally
📋 Applicable Frameworks
🇮🇳 DPDP Act 2023 (India)
Digital Personal Data Protection Act – India's primary data privacy law. Requires explicit consent for processing personal data, data minimization, and breach notification.
Section 4Section 8Section 9
🇪🇺 GDPR (EU/International)
General Data Protection Regulation – If processing data of EU residents. Requires lawful basis for processing, data subject rights, DPA notification within 72 hours.
Article 5Article 25Article 32Article 33
🔒 ISO/IEC 27001:2022
International standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). Covers risk assessment, access control, cryptography, physical security, and incident management.
Clause 6.1A.8A.9
📜 IT Act 2000 + Amendment 2008 (India)
India's primary cybersecurity legislation. Section 43A requires reasonable security practices. Section 66 covers computer-related offenses. CERT-In rules apply.
Section 43ASection 66Rule 3
🏫 Sector-Specific (Education)
📚 UDISE+ Data Guidelines
Ministry of Education guidelines for protecting student data collected under UDISE+. Requires secure storage, restricted access, and data accuracy.
Data ProtectionAccess Control
🛡️ NIST Cybersecurity Framework
NIST CSF 2.0 – Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover. Widely adopted global standard for managing cybersecurity risk across all organization sizes.
IdentifyProtectDetectRespond
☁️ CERT-In Guidelines 2022 (India)
CERT-In mandates incident reporting within 6 hours, maintain logs for 180 days, mandatory cybersecurity drills, and vulnerability management for all organizations.
Incident ReportLog Retention
🔐 PCI-DSS (if payments handled)
Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard – If the organization processes any online payments. Requires encrypted data transmission, access controls, and regular audits.
Req. 3Req. 7Req. 12
